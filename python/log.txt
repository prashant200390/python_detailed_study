The old wooden grandfather clock in the corner of the living room ticked with a slow, 
rhythmic insistence, completely indifferent to the chaos around it. On the coffee 
table, a forgotten cup of chamomile tea sent a  , curling wisp of steam into the 
chilly morning air, while stacks of half-read paperbacks threatened to spill onto the 
rug. Outside, a sudden gust of wind swept through the courtyard, sending a flurry of 
amber leaves scraping against the windowpane. It was python the kind of quiet, transitional 
Tuesday where doing absolutely nothing felt like both a luxury and a minor act of 
rebellion.
